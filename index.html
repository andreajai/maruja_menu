Sobre La Casa de Maruja


From Naranjal, Lima Peru

La Casa de Maruja is a Peruvian bakery with deep roots in a small neighborhood called Naranjal. It all began in the kitchen of La Abuelita Maruja and her 4-year-old granddaughter, Andrea. They enjoyed making "Cachanga" together—a deep-fried bread that pairs beautifully with either Manjar Blanco, jelly, or cheese.

Since then, they love baking a variety of traditional Peruvian treats, including delicious Alfajores, Picarones, Torta Helada, and Empanadas con Limón together. However, their specialty lies in the Street Style, Deep-Fried bread, Cachanga!

This bakery is a tribute to La Abuelita Maruja, her kitchen, and her granddaughter, who loved playing with the dough before it was fried. Most importantly, it celebrates their country, which has taught them the value of delicious food and how it brings people together.

Maruja and Andrea, invite you to be a little curious, and bold by trying one of these unique baked goods that they cherish making with love.
